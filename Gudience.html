Guidance for students

Pet Abandonment

NEVER ABANDON YOUR PET. Abandoning your pet is incredibly cruel,and also illegal under the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act 1979.Penalties apply if you are found guilty of pet abandonment. Due to thetransitory nature of university life, cats are sometimes left behindwhen their owners move away and do not make arrangements fortheir pet. Abandoning your pet will cause it extreme stress.

Living on the street or on campus will be confusing and frighteningfor your pet. Don't assume that your pet will survive when you moveaway and leave it behind. Contrary to popular belief, domestic cats donot automatically return to their "natural" instincts and they find ithard to fend for themselves. If you have a pet you can no longer carefor, it is your legal and moral responsibility to ensure that it has asecure alternative home where it is safe and well cared for. If youare unable to find a responsible home, you may seek to surrender itto your local vet or a no-kill animal shelter.

NB Contact these organisations as early as possible to check whenthey can take your cat.

Companion Animals Act & NSW Companion

Animal Register

The Companion Animals Act was passed by the NSW Government in 1998. Under this Act all kittens born after 1 July 1999 and kept as pets must be permanently identified through micro-chipping by12 weeks of age, and must be regis-tered through alocal council on the NSW Companion Animals Register by 6 months of age. Cats born earlier who change ownership must also be chipped and registered. All UNSW campus cats have been microchipped and registered as owned by CCNSW through Randwick City Council
